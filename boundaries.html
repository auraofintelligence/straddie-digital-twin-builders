Boundaries

The trust layer is part of the prompt architecture.

A digital twin prompt builder that cannot say "this stays private" is not ready to scale. These boundaries protect people, place, culture, health, property conversations, generated visuals and public credibility.

Guardrails

What the public site will not pretend.

No raw private data

Do not render health history, raw biometric data, family context, home routines, access codes, private addresses or identity keys.

No approval theatre

A generated scene is not a council approval, landholder agreement, grant win, partner commitment, clinical endorsement or cultural permission.

No clinical advice

Health visuals can show support logistics and review pathways. Treatment, diagnosis, device operation and therapy claims need qualified review.

No surveillance creep

Public-interest simulation must not become a reason to scrape people, track households or centralise private life.

No cultural flattening

Place data can carry cultural sensitivity. Authority, protocol and correction paths matter more than a neat public map.

No stale status claims

Property, tender, planning, funding and government-source claims need dates and live re-checks before formal use.

Healthy next step

Make the smallest reviewable prompt.

When in doubt, the correct next move is not to render more. Make a private or permissioned .md prompt packet with the uncertainty written clearly, then ask the right human to check it.

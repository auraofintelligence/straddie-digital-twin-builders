L0 private space

The first twin belongs to the person or place steward.

L0 is the home cell: a room, desk, studio, garden bed, capsule, phone, local server, health support note or private memory context. It is where the system learns to respect the owner before it tries to help anyone else.

Questionnaire spine

Plain questions for L0 notes.

These are the questions the builder asks before any mesh, public dashboard or AI agent gets involved.

What is the space?

Name the room, bench, garden, capsule, device cluster or private place in ordinary words.

What should it help with?

Remembering, planning, repair, care, learning, comfort, energy, food, privacy, safety or simulation.

What stays private?

Health context, identity, routines, household details, raw sensor streams, family notes and location-sensitive material.

Who may review it?

The owner, carer, clinician, venue steward, trusted friend, technician or human reviewer.

What can be shared later?

A minimal public summary, aggregated pattern, maintenance need, resource request or anonymous insight.

How can it be corrected?

Pause, delete, replace, withdraw, update source date, change reviewer or sever the sync path.

Aura boundary

Clothing, not skin.

The Aura security notes and capsule compute pages both point to the same rule: a digital twin should remain an external support layer. It can help remember, summarise and simulate, but it should be severable, reviewable and owned by the person it represents.

Capsule support berth

A quiet rest pod with strict privacy, comfort, heat, power and data rules before any idle compute is routed elsewhere.

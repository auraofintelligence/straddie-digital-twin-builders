Why this exists

A digital twin starts as a description a neighbour can understand.

A useful civic twin does not begin with a glowing dashboard. It begins when someone can say what a place is, what it carries, what should stay private, what evidence exists, what would be unsafe to assume, and who gets to correct the record.

Describe before modelling Plain-English scene notes make the first version inspectable before maps, sensors or simulations get fancy.
Boundary before scale L0 data stays private by default. L1 is permissioned. L2 is public only after aggregation, source checks and review.
Simulation before commitment Mirror Country style rehearsal lets people test options before a grant, site decision, public claim or infrastructure push hardens.

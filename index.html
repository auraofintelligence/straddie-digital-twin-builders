Why this exists

A simulation starts as a scene a neighbour can recognise.

A useful visual twin does not begin with a glowing dashboard. It begins when someone can say what should be visible, what should move, what can be customised, what evidence exists, what would be unsafe to invent, and who gets to correct the generated world.

Describe before rendering Plain-English prompt packets make the first visual inspectable before maps, sensors or simulations get fancy.
Boundary before scale Level 0 (L0) stays private by default. Level 1 (L1) is permissioned. Level 2 (L2) uses public-safe and source-checked context.
Simulation before commitment Mirror Country style rehearsal lets people test visuals, routes, crowds, weather and options before a grant, site decision or infrastructure push hardens.

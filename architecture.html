Architecture

Level 0 (L0) private space, Level 1 (L1) place mesh, Level 2 (L2) living boundary.

The Sovereign Space Builder and P4A patterns both point to the same practical ladder: prompt the private scene first, then the trusted local world, then the public or bioregional simulation only when the context has earned that scale.

L0

Level 0 (L0) private scene

A room, home, studio, capsule, garden, device cluster or personal object prompt. It is owned by the person or steward closest to it. Raw health, identity, household, sensor and emotional context stays out of generated visuals by default.

L1

Level 1 (L1) trusted place world

A venue, street, club, Ballow Road hub, maker-space, kiosk, noticeboard, event crew or local project. Level 1 (L1) is where selected local context becomes a scene that can be rendered, walked through, customised or rehearsed.

L2

Level 2 (L2) living boundary simulation

The island, a town network, a civic front, a catchment or the Moreton Bay watershed. Level 2 (L2) should receive public-source patterns and reviewable context, not raw private lives.

Builder loop

Observe, prompt, render, review, customise.

This is the plain-English version of the technical stack: CRDTs, DIDs, Verifiable Credentials and GraphRAG matter later. The first move is more humble: make a clear .md prompt packet with a scene, scale, camera, style, source hints, customisation controls and negative prompt.

01

Observe

Look at the scene without forcing a solution. What is the room, place, route or boundary actually carrying?

02

Prompt

Turn the observation into a Markdown prompt packet with ordinary language, a source date and a scale label.

03

Render

Send the packet to a visual generator, video model, 3D world builder, game prototype or agent simulation.

04

Review

Ask what the generator got wrong, what it invented, and what should stay private or approval-only.

05

Customise

Adjust weather, crowd level, camera angle, screen state, route, lighting, material detail and scenario pressure.

Plain translation

What the stack means without jargon.

Offline-first

The prompt packet should remain useful on a laptop, phone or kiosk even if the internet is patchy.

Consent first

The person or steward closest to the scene decides what visual detail can move outward.

Accuracy without oversharing

Share enough to guide a generator without exposing private details or fake certainty.

Correction path

Every generated scene needs a way to fix wrong scale, wrong geography, wrong culture, wrong status or invented detail.

Simulation is rehearsal

The purpose is better choices before pressure, not a fantasy dashboard.

Scale is earned

Level 2 (L2) simulations should come from reviewed Level 0 (L0) and Level 1 (L1) context, not from scraping private life.

Architecture

L0 private space, L1 place mesh, L2 living boundary.

The Sovereign Space Builder and P4A patterns both point to the same practical ladder: describe the private node first, then the trusted local mesh, then the public or bioregional layer only when the data has earned that scale.

L0

Private space node

A room, home, studio, capsule, garden, device cluster or personal place note. It is owned by the person or steward closest to it. Raw health, identity, household, sensor and emotional context stays here by default.

L1

Trusted place mesh

A venue, street, club, Ballow Road hub, maker-space, kiosk, noticeboard, event crew or local project. L1 is where specific people can share selected context for a job: a rehearsal, repair, public notice, intake note or compatibility check.

L2

Living boundary map

The island, a town network, a civic front, a catchment or the Moreton Bay watershed. L2 should receive summaries, patterns, public sources and reviewable claims, not raw private lives.

Builder loop

Observe, describe, review, simulate, publish only what is ready.

This is the plain-English version of the technical stack: CRDTs, DIDs, Verifiable Credentials and GraphRAG matter later. The first move is more humble: make a clear .md file with a boundary, purpose, sources, review gate and correction path.

01

Observe

Look at the scene without forcing a solution. What is the room, place, route or boundary actually carrying?

02

Describe

Turn the observation into a Markdown file with ordinary language and a source date.

03

Review

Ask who can check it, who can correct it, and what should stay private or approval-only.

04

Simulate

Use the note to rehearse a practical question: visitor flow, repair path, kiosk fallback, health surge, market night, ferry pressure.

05

Publish Carefully

Share only public-safe summaries and links. Keep raw data, private identity and uncertain claims behind the right gate.

Plain translation

What the stack means without jargon.

Offline-first

The note should remain useful on a laptop, phone or kiosk even if the internet is patchy.

Consent first

The person or steward closest to the data decides what can move outward.

Proof without oversharing

Where possible, share enough to verify a claim without exposing private details.

Correction path

Every public record needs a way to fix stale, wrong, disputed or withdrawn material.

Simulation is rehearsal

The purpose is better choices before pressure, not a fantasy dashboard.

Scale is earned

L2 summaries should come from reviewed L0/L1 material, not from scraping private life.
